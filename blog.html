Eat like a local

A First Timer's Guide to Ordering Filipino Food in Nampa

By the kitchen at Authentic Taste of the Philippines · Nampa, Idaho

Listen: Ava and Leo talk Filipino food
A two minute guide for first timers

If you have driven past the Asian grocery store on Caldwell Boulevard, you have driven past one of the best meals in Nampa without knowing it. Authentic Taste of the Philippines is a small, family run kitchen run by Auntie Juliet and the ladies who cook every order by hand. Filipino food does not always get the spotlight in the Treasure Valley, so if you have never tried it, this guide will help you order like a regular on your very first visit.

Start with lumpia, always

Every Filipino meal has a beginning, and that beginning is lumpia. These are thin egg rolls, rolled by hand and fried to order so the wrapper shatters when you bite it. We make both meat lumpia and vegetable lumpia, and at a dollar fifty a piece they are easy to order by the handful. Get more than you think you need. They disappear off the table faster than anything else, and they are the single best introduction to the kitchen. Dip them, share them, and do not be surprised when someone reaches for your last one.

Build your plate around a rice meal

Once the lumpia is going, anchor your order with a rice meal. The one regulars come back for is lechon kawali, pork belly that is simmered until tender and then deep fried so the skin turns to crackling while the inside stays juicy. It comes with a generous scoop of steamed rice for eighteen dollars, and it is the plate that converts first timers into regulars. If you are feeling celebratory, ask whether the crispy pata is on that day. It is our show stopper special, a whole deep fried pork hock with shattering skin, and it is made for sharing.

Do not skip the pancit

Pancit is stir fried noodles tossed with vegetables and savory bits, and in Filipino culture it means long life, which is why it shows up at every birthday and gathering. At ten dollars it is the easiest thing on the menu to share, and it rounds out a table beautifully next to a few sticks of lumpia. If you are ordering for a group, a plate of pancit and a couple dozen lumpia is the move. It feeds everyone, it pleases the picky eaters, and it tastes like a fiesta.

Ask about the specials

Here is the insider tip that separates the regulars from the first timers: the board on the wall is only part of the story. The kitchen cooks home style specials that rotate with the day, and the reviews are full of them. Chicken adobo, the savory soy and vinegar braise that is the unofficial national dish. Sisig, sizzling chopped pork with a citrus kick. Dinuguan, the rich pork stew that the adventurous swear by. Beef mechado simmered in tomato sauce until it falls apart. These are not always written down, so the smartest thing you can do is call ahead at (208) 250-7630 and ask what is cooking. Whatever the answer is, order it.

Save room for siopao and something sweet

Between meals, Filipinos do merienda, an afternoon snack break, and siopao is the classic. These are soft, pillowy steamed buns filled with slow cooked savory pork, warm and filling and perfect to take to go. Finish sweet with turon, a sugar and plantain eggroll fried until the wrapper caramelizes into a candy shell, and an ube shake. Ube is purple yam, and the shake is thick, creamy, and an unmistakable bright purple. It is the thing everyone photographs, and for good reason. Real yam, no fake flavor, pure Filipino comfort in a cup.

Why locals keep coming back

What makes this place special is not just the food, although the food is the real thing. It is the welcome. Reviewers from the Filipino community write that eating here feels like visiting family, that the ladies treat you like their own, and that if you are homesick, this is the cure. A four point nine rating across nearly a hundred Google reviews does not happen by accident. It happens when a small kitchen cooks with heart and treats every guest like a kababayan.

How to visit

Authentic Taste of the Philippines is at 433 Caldwell Boulevard in Nampa, right next to the Asian grocery store, so you can grab a bite before or after you shop. They are open Tuesday through Saturday from eleven in the morning to six in the evening, and closed Sunday and Monday. Dine in or call ahead for curbside pickup. Bring your appetite, bring a friend, and come hungry for home.
